## Landmark-day spot-check (the red flag)

| Date | Assigned SWT | Expected | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black Saturday 2009-02-07 | AM-E | FH/TH hot northerly | QUESTIONABLE |
| Ash Wednesday 1983-02-16 | WH-A | FH-family | plausible, weaker than expected |
| TC Yasi landfall 2011-02-03 | AM-E | active monsoon | SOLID |
| Black Summer 2019-12-31 | TH-C | blocking high east | SOLID |
| Black Summer 2020-01-04 | AM-B | hot NW over SE | QUESTIONABLE |

Two of three archetypal SE hot-northerly catastrophes classify as AM
(active monsoon). Either AM family labels are misleading shorthand for
multi-mode circulation clusters, or the classifier confuses tropical
northerly surges with mid-latitude hot northerlies. **Consequence for
EM_Demand:** the AM-E demand enrichment (RR 1.52) may partly contain
misfiled southern fire-catastrophe days. The SWT classifier itself was
trusted as ground truth here and deserves a separate audit. Until then:
cite the danger RR and the null conversion; treat AM-flavoured results
with caution.

## Machinery checks

- Month-matching in `simultaneity_rr` is genuine (background resampled
  within calendar month, matching the month distribution of multi days).
- Period-matching 1979–2023 enforced in notebook cell 7 (derived
  intersection, filtered before all tables) — effective, though an explicit
  assert against the FFDI bounds would be safer.
- FDR is BH per table (30 SWTs), not across the whole study (fire, danger,
  pairs, conversion, spread jointly) — defensible but disclose it.
- Fixed-numerator bootstrap (only the background resampled) is an
  enrichment-CI construction, not a permutation test; reproduces as
  designed; philosophical critique, not a bug.
- Block-bootstrap sensitivity (cell 15) is a genuine strength.

## Top defence-attack vectors

1. Burn-window imputation drives the multi-state fire count (see above);
   the ignition-only variant should arguably be the headline.
2. State area-mean FFDI ≥ monthly p90 as "danger day": a high bar for
   WA/QLD (favours the signal), but VIC means are mallee-dominated and TAS
   is noise-dominated (mean FFDI 2.7, p99 = 11.6) — pair results involving
   TAS are shaky.
3. Black Saturday / 2020-01-04 as AM (above) — demands a case-study
   appendix or a classifier audit.
